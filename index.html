Sapta Swaras — सप्त स्वर
The Seven Divine Sounds — Cosmic Bridge between Music & Mantra
Philosophical Basis
The Sapta Swaras are not merely musical notes but manifestations of Nada Brahman — the primordial sound of universal consciousness. Each swara is a living vibration carrying deity-consciousness.
Yogic Significance
Each of the seven swaras corresponds to a specific chakra, activating pranic flow through the sushumna nadi when sung correctly. Sa-Ri-Ga-Ma-Pa-Dha-Ni maps to Muladhara through Sahasrara.
Vedic Origin
The Samaveda is the song of the Vedas — it prescribes swaras for every Rigvedic mantra. The Gandharva Veda details how swaras connect cosmic order (rita) with musical order (swara).
Connection to Lalitha
Devi Lalitha is described in the Sahasranamavali as residing in the Nada — "Nada-rupa" — being the very form of sound. Her 1000 names traverse the entire spectrum of divine vibration.
Sources: shlokam.org/lalitha-sahasranamam · vedadhara.com · vedicheritage.gov.in

About Nada Neerajanam
The Vision, Mission and Knowledge Architecture
नाद नीराजनम् — Waving the Lamp of Sound

Nada Neerajanam is a sacred knowledge portal dedicated to establishing the profound connection between the Sapta Swaras (seven musical notes) and the great Vedic texts — Lalitha Sahasranamavali, Devi Khadgamala Stotram, and the Shri Rudram (Namakam and Chamakam). The name itself means "offering of sound as light" — as the priest waves an oil lamp before the deity, we offer the lamp of musical consciousness before the divine.

The portal provides a two-phased application: Phase 1 operates without API integration, directing users to the finest Vedic databases for raw textual research. Phase 2 offers AI-assisted composition, cross-referencing, and 6-viewpoint analysis. Together they create an unprecedented bridge between ancient sacred science and contemporary research tools.
